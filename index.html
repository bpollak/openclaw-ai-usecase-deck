Personal AI
Architecture
Building one that actually knows you
Brett Pollak · Executive Director, Workplace Technology
UC San Diego
The Problem

Generic AI is generic.
Useful AI knows your world.

Ask any chatbot “who should I follow up with this week?” → generic productivity advice.
Ask one that knows your calendar, your meetings, your projects, your campus → answers that move work forward.

The gap isn’t model intelligence. The big models are smart enough.
The gap is knowledge — and the right context at the right moment.

Most personal-AI projects stop at “connect to my email.” That’s table stakes.
The interesting work is what comes next: capture, synthesize, retrieve.

The Architecture

One pipeline, three components

Raw signals flow left → right through accumulation and synthesis crons into the durable knowledge layer,
where the agent pulls them back out at the moment of conversation.
Context Loading

Three layers — context is a budget, not a pantry

Layer 1 always-loaded (~15 KB) · Layer 2 triggered on conversation (190 wiki pages) · Layer 3 explicit deep-dive
The Pipeline

46 cron jobs on a predictable daily rhythm

Morning accumulation → evening synthesis → night consolidation → Sunday cross-day pattern extraction
The Durable Layer

Knowledge graph + wiki — brain stem and cortex

Knowledge Graph
201 nodes · 244 edges
People, technologies, vendors, decisions, projects.
Edges: meets_with, often_meets_with, uses.
Fast, shallow, relational — “who works on X.”
Wiki
190 narrative pages
Markdown pages mirroring high-weight entities.
Auto-promoted from graph; enriched by meetings + manual edits.
Slow, rich, narrative — “why did we choose X.”
Plus a nightly Confluence sync brings in 2,500+ pages from 9 UCSD ITS spaces
— runbooks, team docs, AI governance — so the agent sees what the org has written down.
The Inference Layer

Model allocation — matching jobs to capabilities

45 of 46 enabled jobs run on TritonAI — UCSD’s institutional gateway, on-prem at SDSC.
One job stays on OpenAI Codex as a cross-provider canary.

Outcomes

What the system actually delivers

⏱ 2–3 hours/week saved
Research, prep, triage, news scanning, follow-up tracking. The crons gather; I do the judgment.
📈 Pattern recognition I’d miss
“VPN failed 5 of 7 days” doesn’t surface in daily reports. Weekly synthesis surfaces it.
🧠 Institutional memory
“Why did we pick this over that?” has an answer. “When did we last discuss X?” has a date.
🎯 Never starting from zero
Every conversation begins with 48 hours of context, curated long-term memory, triggered wiki lookups.
💡 Opportunities surfaced proactively
Opportunity scanner + AI news digest find campus projects matching priorities. 60 sec to review.
↻ Cross-session continuity
Decisions write back to wiki. Patterns write to PATTERNS.md. Future sessions inherit what present sessions learn.
Forward-Looking

What else becomes possible — once the foundation is in place

📋 Meeting prep automation
Auto-compile attendee wiki pages, recent interactions, open commitments, and a one-page prep doc before every meeting. No more walking in cold.
🔔 Proactive nudges
“This problem has come up three weeks in a row — here are the options you’ve already explored.” The agent notices patterns without being asked.
✍️ Draft-first communications
Emails and memos drafted in your voice, grounded in your actual context — not generic AI writing. Review beats drafting from scratch.
🏛️ Decision archaeology
“Why did we pick this architecture two years ago?” → immediate answer with full reasoning, the voices that disagreed, the tradeoffs.

Most of these are implementation work, not research. The hard part — the knowledge layer — is built.

The Vision

Scaling to UC San Diego — what if every staff member had one?

Privacy is the precondition. Personal data never leaves the personal agent.
Each tier is opt-in, anonymized, or aggregate-only — never individual records flowing upward.
The Real Question
This isn’t
“can AI do this?”

The technology mostly exists. The hard problem is design, governance, and procurement.
What would it take to do this responsibly, at scale, in a way a 30,000-employee university can actually adopt?

Every AI vendor is converging on the same lock-in: a single app turning chat into an agent, using your context as the moat. Switch vendors, lose it.
Knowledge in your filesystem, in open formats, under your control flips that.
The context layer is the moat — and it doesn’t belong to the vendor.
